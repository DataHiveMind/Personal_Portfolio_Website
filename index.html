Research Thesis

Financial decision systems are partially observed and non-stationary, so pure return maximization is insufficient. My thesis is that robust RL requires explicit risk constraints, stress-tested environments, and evaluation protocols that survive regime shifts.

  • Problem Framing: Formulate trading and execution as constrained sequential control with realistic costs.
  • Primary Objective: Optimize risk-adjusted performance, not just raw return.
  • Validation Standard: Prioritize out-of-sample stability, distributional diagnostics, and stress behavior.

Research Interserts

My workflow mirrors a rigorous research cycle: define assumptions, build environments, train agents, and invalidate findings before iteration.

  • Environment Design: Encode transaction costs, slippage, latency effects, and position constraints.
  • Validation Discipline: Apply rolling, regime-split, and distributional backtesting with baseline comparisons.
  • Failure Analysis: Diagnose instability during volatility shocks, spread widening, and correlation breakdowns.
