Research Thesis

Financial markets are partially observed, regime-dependent systems where naive reward maximization fails. My research direction is to train agents that remain stable under regime changes by combining robust RL, uncertainty-aware evaluation, and execution-constrained environments.

  • Problem Framing: Treat alpha generation and hedging as sequential decision processes with explicit costs and inventory constraints.
  • Primary Objective: Maximize risk-adjusted returns while controlling drawdown sensitivity and turnover.
  • Research Standard: Prioritize out-of-sample robustness over in-sample peak metrics.

Research Methodology

My workflow mirrors a quant research loop: formalize assumptions, construct environment dynamics, train policies, and invalidate aggressively before iterating.

  • Environment Design: Include transaction costs, slippage proxies, liquidity limits, and delayed reward structures.
  • Validation Discipline: Use rolling-window and regime-split backtests to detect overfitting and strategy decay.
  • Failure Analysis: Inspect policy behavior during volatility spikes and abnormal spread expansion to locate fragility.
