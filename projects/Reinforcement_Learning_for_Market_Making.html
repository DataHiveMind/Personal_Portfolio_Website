Reinforcement Learning for Market Making

DQN + PPO Agents · Inventory Control · MLflow Tracking

A quantitative research framework for market making that learns bid-ask and inventory decisions with deep reinforcement learning, while enforcing risk controls through inventory penalties, position limits, and drawdown-aware evaluation.

Agents

Multi-agent framework with DQN and PPO policies for trading decisions.

Data

Minute-level AAPL data with 50+ engineered market and technical features.

Goal

Maximize risk-adjusted returns while controlling inventory risk and drawdown.

Research Methodology

The environment and training loop are designed to mirror realistic market making constraints.

  • State Space: Market features + inventory + cash + portfolio value.
  • Action Space: Discrete actions {sell_big, sell_small, hold, buy_small, buy_big}.
  • Reward Design: PnL delta with inventory penalty term to reduce position risk.
  • Risk Controls: Position limits, transaction costs, slippage, and drawdown constraints.

Experimental Results

Out-of-Sample Backtest

  • DQN: 12.5% return, Sharpe 1.84, Max Drawdown -8.2%
  • PPO: 14.2% return, Sharpe 2.01, Max Drawdown -6.9%
  • Buy & Hold: 8.7% return, Sharpe 1.22, Max Drawdown -15.3%
Risk-Adjusted Outperformance vs Baselines

Training + Validation

  • DQN converged near 150 episodes with stable learning dynamics.
  • PPO achieved strong consistency with lower variance in returns.
  • MLflow tracked experiments, hyperparameters, and metric history.

Key Charts

DQN Training Metrics

This training-curve visualization tracks reward dynamics and stability as the DQN policy learns market-making behavior. Convergence shape provides a direct view into whether exploration, replay, and target updates are producing controlled policy improvement. Plateau behavior near later episodes suggests diminishing marginal gains and supports an evidence-based early-stopping or retuning decision. The figure is useful for recruiters and researchers because it ties implementation quality to measurable learning behavior rather than only final backtest numbers.

Risk-Adjusted Strategy Comparison

This bar chart summarizes risk-adjusted performance across trained RL agents and baseline strategies using Sharpe ratio. PPO leads the set, while DQN also outperforms classical benchmark approaches under the same evaluation framing. The relative spacing between bars indicates that gains are not only return-driven but tied to volatility-aware consistency. In a research workflow, this chart acts as a compact decision aid for model selection before deeper stress testing and transaction-cost sensitivity checks.
