Project Case Study · RL Execution + Market Microstructure

Reinforcement Learning Agent for Intraday Execution Optimization

Double DQN · Limit Order Book Simulation · VaR/CVaR Analytics

This project develops an intraday execution RL framework with realistic latency and slippage effects, then evaluates PnL distributions with Monte Carlo simulation and tail-risk metrics across multiple large-cap tech tickers.

Agent Design

Double DQN with replay buffer, target-network sync, and epsilon-greedy exploration.

Execution Realism

Simulated limit-order-book behavior includes latency sampling and slippage costs.

Risk Lens

Monte Carlo PnL, VaR, and CVaR are used to evaluate downside behavior.

Methodology

The training and evaluation flow is designed for execution-quality optimization under intraday market uncertainty.

  • Environment: Gym-compatible execution simulator with market frictions.
  • Learning Setup: Double DQN with experience replay and target updates for stability.
  • Data Regime: Real tick data with multi-ticker evaluation coverage.
  • Risk Evaluation: Repeated simulation for distribution-aware performance metrics.

Key Charts

AAPL Monte Carlo PnL Distribution

This distribution chart shows the spread of PnL outcomes from repeated simulation under the trained execution policy. The concentration around a positive mean with bounded left tail indicates that latency and slippage controls are functioning as intended for this instrument. Dispersion width gives an intuitive view of execution uncertainty that single-run backtests cannot capture. As a research artifact, the figure supports probabilistic decision-making around capital allocation and live deployment thresholds.

NVDA Monte Carlo PnL Distribution

This NVDA distribution illustrates a materially weaker profile and highlights ticker-dependent execution difficulty. The left-shifted center and lower VaR boundary indicate that microstructure differences can degrade policy quality when market conditions change. Comparing this against stronger tickers demonstrates why cross-asset validation is necessary before production rollout. The chart motivates adaptive policy tuning and regime-conditioned execution logic rather than fixed single-policy assumptions.
