Project Case Study · Reinforcement Learning + Portfolio Allocation

Hierarchical DRL Multi-Strategy Fund

7 Specialist Trading Agents + 1 Master CIO Allocator

This project implements a hierarchical deep reinforcement learning framework where specialist agents run distinct strategies and a master PPO agent dynamically allocates capital based on current market conditions and specialist behavior.

Core Result

Master CIO Sharpe Ratio of 1.60 over the 2020-2024 test period.

Risk Profile

Maximum drawdown of -0.80%, materially lower than benchmark allocations.

Research Focus

Adaptive capital allocation with explicit transaction costs and slippage assumptions.

System Architecture

The hierarchy separates tactical signal generation from portfolio-level risk-aware allocation.

Specialist Layer (7 Agents)

  • Statistical Arbitrage (DDPG)
  • Market Making (DQN)
  • Factor Tracking (PPO)
  • Volatility Trading (DDPG)
  • Delta Hedging (DQN)
  • Futures Spreads (PPO)
  • FX Arbitrage (DDPG)
Specialized Policy Behaviors

Master CIO Layer

  • Algorithm: PPO
  • Input: Specialist metrics + market state
  • Output: Dynamic allocation weights
  • Objective: Optimize risk-adjusted portfolio performance

Key Charts

Equity Curves Comparison

This chart compares cumulative performance across the master CIO policy and benchmark allocations over the same test horizon. The master layer shows smoother compounding than high-volatility alternatives despite lower absolute peaks. The relative path stability supports the thesis that adaptive allocation can improve robustness under non-stationary conditions. From a research standpoint, the figure demonstrates that risk-adjusted consistency is achieved without relying on a single specialist strategy.

Drawdown Comparison

This drawdown plot visualizes peak-to-trough losses for each allocation method and highlights downside control. The hierarchical policy maintains substantially shallower drawdown depth than equal-weight and ensemble alternatives during stress windows. The duration of underwater periods is also reduced, which is critical for practical capital retention and redeployment. The figure supports the central claim that dynamic specialist weighting improves portfolio resilience rather than only maximizing returns.
