Intelligent Risk Analytics for Retail Options Trading

HMM Regime Detection · GARCH Volatility Forecasting · VaR/CVaR

A quantitative research framework for retail options risk management across equities, ETFs, and sector ETFs, focused on regime-awareness, volatility clustering, and tail-risk control.

Asset Coverage

26 assets analyzed across equities, broad ETFs, and sector rotation instruments.

Regime Model

3 latent market regimes: low volatility, medium volatility, and crisis/high volatility.

Risk Outcome

Sharpe ratios above 1.5 on selected assets with detailed drawdown and tail-risk analysis.

Methodology

The pipeline combines market-state inference, conditional volatility forecasting, and strategy-level risk diagnostics.

  • Regime Detection: Hidden Markov Models on returns, volatility, and volume features.
  • Volatility Forecasting: GARCH(1,1) estimation for persistent volatility and shock response.
  • Tail Risk Models: Historical VaR, Parametric VaR, and Conditional VaR (Expected Shortfall).
  • Strategy Testing: Moving Average, Momentum, and Mean Reversion across multiple assets.

Key Charts

GARCH Volatility Forecast

This figure shows conditional volatility estimates over time and highlights clustering behavior typical in financial returns. The model captures persistence and shock response, which improves downstream risk calibration relative to static-volatility assumptions. Elevated variance bands align with stress periods, supporting the regime-aware interpretation in the broader framework. For execution and portfolio sizing, the chart provides an interpretable signal for tightening exposure during high-risk windows.

VaR Method Comparison

This comparison plot contrasts historical VaR, parametric VaR, and conditional VaR under the same confidence assumptions. Conditional VaR extends beyond threshold loss and better reflects tail severity during market dislocations. The gap between methods illustrates why relying on one risk lens can understate adverse outcomes in non-Gaussian return distributions. Together, these estimates form a practical risk stack for both research validation and portfolio risk governance.
